Impact of Toxic Release Sites in Pierce County

The Environmental Protection Agency annually collects and publishes information related to toxic chemical releases in the United States. Toxic chemicals like arsenic and lead can pose significant cognitive, and physical health risks, especially to children. From an environmental justice standpoint, it is crucial to assess and address the impact toxic chemicals have on marginalized communities. This interactive webmap represents the impact of toxic chemical releases on multi-family and subsidized housing parcels in Pierce County. The impact is evaluated using multi-ring buffers with .5 mile intervals and a max of 2 miles away from the toxic sites. Parcels within the buffer could be negatively impacted (black). Parcels out of it are less affected (turquoise). More than half of the parcels on that webmap are located within 2 miles of a toxic site.
